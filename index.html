Ferrari Engineering

Where obsession
becomes precision.

Every Ferrari is the result of thousands of engineering hours, wind tunnel testing, and a relentless pursuit of the perfect balance between raw power and surgical control.

Twin-Turbo V8 Architecture
Ferrari's 3.9-litre twin-turbocharged V8 produces 640cv at 7,500rpm — a masterclass in forced induction, delivering relentless torque from as low as 3,000rpm without a hint of lag.

Side-Slip Control 6.1
The latest generation SSC 6.1 system reads 100 vehicle parameters per second, distributing torque between axles in real time to keep the car pinned to the driver's intended line.

Carbon Fibre Everywhere
From the roof panel and rear diffuser to the seat shells and dashboard spine — every gram of carbon fibre is laid by hand, achieving structural rigidity that no metal substitute can match.

Aerodynamic Intelligence
Ferrari's active aerodynamic package generates up to 360kg of downforce at 200km/h. Adaptive flaps and sculpted underbody channels work in concert — invisible forces that keep you planted.

The Sound of Maranello
The exhaust note is engineered as meticulously as the engine itself. Titanium headers, tuned resonance chambers, and a flat-plane crankshaft produce a wail that is unmistakably, irreversibly Ferrari.
